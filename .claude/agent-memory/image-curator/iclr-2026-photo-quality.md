Default expectation for the ICLR 2026 oral-session photos (Day 1 LLM Reasoning session, folders under `src/content/journal/iclr-2026/`):

**Almost all in-session photos are rotated 90° (landscape-captured phone held portrait), angled from a low audience position, with heavy lens flare from stage spotlights and ceiling HVAC ducts visible at the edges.** Slide content reads through but nothing is camera-ready. Expect scores in the 40-60 range by default.

**Why:** Rafael was shooting handheld from the audience. Don't recommend in-session slide photos against a clean paper figure unless the user photo is doing narrative work the figure can't (speaker at podium, room shot, a moment).

**How to apply:** For the ICLR 2026 oral-session folders specifically, default to the paper's Figure 1 or architecture figure when a section is explaining the technique. Reserve user photos for atmospheric/narrative beats (opening, closing, a "here's the room" transition).

**Known exception:** `src/content/journal/iclr-2026/Revela/20260423_112002.jpg` is upright, non-rotated, and clean — it shows the Revela title slide with the speaker at the podium. It's the one photo in the whole session that photographs well. Good fallback for a narrative beat in any section of a Revela-focused post, or as a closing atmosphere shot.

**Known venue shot:** `src/content/journal/iclr-2026/the_challenges_of_human_centered_AI/20260423_081146_3dce473d.jpg` is a crisp pre-session hall photo, but it's scoped to the Mataric-keynote post. Don't reuse without explicit permission.
