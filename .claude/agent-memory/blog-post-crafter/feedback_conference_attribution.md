---
name: Conference-post attribution rule
description: Keynote/talk summaries must clearly attribute ideas to the speaker; Rafael's voice lives in selection and connections, not ownership.
type: feedback
---

When a post is based on someone else's talk (keynote, invited talk, tutorial), the framing must be unambiguous: this is Rafael's **notes and reflections on a talk by [Speaker, affiliation]**, not Rafael's original ideas.

**Why:** The raw journal notes capture Rafael's summary of the speaker's argument. Presenting those summaries as Rafael's own thesis would misattribute the work and misrepresent the post. This was made explicit during the ICLR 2026 / Maja Mataric keynote post.

**How to apply:**
- Open by situating the reader — name the venue, the speaker, their affiliation, the talk title. Ideally inside a scene, not a list.
- Throughout the body, attribute specific claims to the speaker ("she argued", "her framing", "the slide said"). Use verbatim phrasing when quoting and mark it as such.
- Rafael's voice shows up in: what he chose to highlight, what he cut, the connections he draws between ideas, the lines he calls out as especially sharp, the homework he takes away.
- Silently correct typos from the raw notes in the final post ("Greated" → "Greater", "Phsyically" → "Physically", etc.).
